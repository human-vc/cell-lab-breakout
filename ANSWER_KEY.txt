ROOM 1 — CHEMISTRY OF LIFE
Puzzle: Macromolecule Vault
Students drag 4 molecules into their correct macromolecule bins:
- Glucose -> Carbohydrate (monomer of starch/glycogen/cellulose)
- Triglyceride -> Lipid (glycerol + 3 fatty acids)
- Keratin -> Protein (polymer of amino acids joined by peptide bonds)
- mRNA -> Nucleic Acid (polymer of ribonucleotides joined by phosphodiester bonds)

Final Question: Which polysaccharide is the primary energy-storage carbohydrate in animal liver and muscle cells?
Answer: glycogen -> letter G

Alignment: AP Bio LO SYI-1.B, structure and function of macromolecules. Glycogen is a branched alpha-1,4 / alpha-1,6 glucose polymer stored in vertebrate liver and skeletal muscle.


ROOM 2 — CELL STRUCTURE & FUNCTION
Puzzle: Endomembrane Maze
Click organelles in the secretory pathway order: Nucleus -> Ribosome -> Rough ER -> Golgi Apparatus -> Vesicle. Decoys (mitochondrion, lysosome, chloroplast) reset the path if clicked.

Final Question: Type the abbreviation of the organelle where the polypeptide is first inserted (2 letters).
Answer: ER (endoplasmic reticulum), accepting either "ER" or "RER" -> letter E

Alignment: AP Bio LO SYI-1.D, endomembrane system and vesicular transport. Rough ER is studded with ribosomes; newly translated polypeptides enter the lumen co-translationally.


ROOM 3 — CELLULAR ENERGETICS
Puzzle: ATP Audit
Enter net ATP yields per glucose:
- Glycolysis: 2 ATP (cytosol)
- Pyruvate Oxidation + Krebs: 2 ATP (matrix)
- Electron Transport + Chemiosmosis: ~28 ATP (inner mitochondrial membrane). Accepts 26-34; textbooks vary depending on NADH shuttle efficiency.
- Total: ~32 ATP

Final Question: The primary electron carrier that delivers high-energy electrons to the ETC is...
Answer: NADH -> letter N

Alignment: AP Bio LO ENE-1.I / ENE-1.J, ATP synthesis via chemiosmosis. NADH donates electrons to Complex I; FADH2 to Complex II (~3 vs ~2 ATP per molecule).


ROOM 4 — CELL COMMUNICATION & CELL CYCLE
Puzzle: Signal Cascade
Drag the 5 steps into the correct order for a G-protein-coupled receptor (GPCR) cascade, using epinephrine -> glycogen breakdown:
1. Epinephrine (ligand) binds to GPCR on cell surface
2. Receptor changes shape and activates a G-protein inside the cell
3. Activated G-protein activates adenylyl cyclase, producing cAMP (second messenger)
4. cAMP activates Protein Kinase A, which phosphorylates enzymes
5. Glycogen phosphorylase breaks down glycogen -> glucose released

Final Question: General name for a biological catalyst that lowers activation energy?
Answer: enzyme -> letter E

Alignment: AP Bio LO IST-3.A, signal transduction pathways. The cascade illustrates signal amplification, where one hormone molecule can activate thousands of PKA molecules.


ROOM 5 — HEREDITY
Puzzle: Punnett Safe
Complete a Punnett square for Pp x pp:
- Offspring genotypes: Pp, Pp, pp, pp
- Phenotype ratio: 1 purple : 1 white (1:1)

Final Question: A cross between an organism with a dominant phenotype (unknown genotype) and a homozygous recessive individual is called a...
Answer: testcross -> letter T

Alignment: AP Bio LO IST-1.I, Mendelian inheritance. A 1:1 ratio in the offspring reveals the unknown parent was heterozygous; a 1:0 ratio (all dominant) would indicate homozygous dominant.


ROOM 6 — GENE EXPRESSION & REGULATION
Puzzle: Splice & Decode
The pre-mRNA contains 5 exons and 3 introns (introns start with GU and end with AG per the classic GT-AG rule). Students click each intron to remove it.
- Exons to keep: AUG, GAU, AAU, GCA, UAA
- Introns to remove: GUAAAG, GUACAG, GUAUAG (each begins with 5'-GU and ends with AG-3')
- Mature mRNA: AUG-GAU-AAU-GCA-UAA
- Amino acids: Met - Asp (D) - Asn (N) - Ala (A) - STOP, which spells "D-N-A" (Easter egg)

Final Question: The non-coding sequences removed from pre-mRNA are called...
Answer: introns -> letter I

Alignment: AP Bio LO IST-1.K / IST-1.L, RNA processing. The spliceosome, a ribonucleoprotein complex, removes introns and ligates exons to form the mature mRNA before export from the nucleus.


ROOM 7 — NATURAL SELECTION
Puzzle: Evolutionary Tree
Given a trait matrix with 5 species, pick the correct tree. Traits are cumulative: Lancelet (outgroup, no traits) -> Lamprey (+vertebrae) -> Salamander (+four limbs) -> Lizard (+amniotic egg) -> Mouse (+hair). The correct tree nests each new clade inside the previous.
Correct tree: Tree A (nested clades matching synapomorphies)

Final Question: A branching diagram showing evolutionary relationships based on shared derived traits is a...
Answer: cladogram -> letter C

Alignment: AP Bio LO EVO-3.A, constructing phylogenetic trees and cladograms. Parsimony principle: the simplest tree (fewest evolutionary changes) is preferred when multiple are consistent with the data.


ROOM 8 — ECOLOGY
Puzzle: Ecosystem Reset
Order stages of primary succession (post-volcanic eruption):
1. Bare rock exposed by eruption
2. Lichens and mosses colonize and soil begins forming (pioneer species)
3. Grasses and shrubs establish in soil
4. Mature forest community (climax)

Final Question: The gradual process of community change leading toward a stable climax community is ecological...
Answer: succession -> letter S

Alignment: AP Bio LO SYI-3.F, ecological community dynamics. Primary succession begins without soil (rock, glaciated surfaces); secondary succession begins after disturbance to existing soil (fire, flood, logging).
