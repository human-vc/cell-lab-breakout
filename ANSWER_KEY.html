Room 1, Chemistry of Life

Puzzle: Macromolecule Vault

Students drag 4 molecules into their correct macromolecule bins:

  • Glucose → Carbohydrate (monomer of starch/glycogen/cellulose)
  • Triglyceride → Lipid (glycerol + 3 fatty acids)
  • Keratin → Protein (polymer of amino acids joined by peptide bonds)
  • mRNA → Nucleic Acid (polymer of ribonucleotides joined by phosphodiester bonds)

Final Question

Which polysaccharide is the primary energy-storage carbohydrate in animal liver and muscle cells?

Answer: glycogen → letter G

Alignment with AP Bio LO SYI-1.B, Structure & function of macromolecules. Glycogen is a branched α-1,4 / α-1,6 glucose polymer stored in vertebrate liver and skeletal muscle.

Room 2, Cell Structure & Function

Puzzle: Endomembrane Maze

Click organelles in the secretory pathway order: Nucleus → Ribosome → Rough ER → Golgi Apparatus → Vesicle. Decoys (mitochondrion, lysosome, chloroplast) reset the path if clicked.

Final Question

Type the abbreviation of the organelle where the polypeptide is first inserted (2 letters).

Answer: ER (endoplasmic reticulum), accepting either "ER" or "RER" → letter E

Alignment with AP Bio LO SYI-1.D, Endomembrane system & vesicular transport. Rough ER is studded with ribosomes; newly translated polypeptides enter the lumen co-translationally.

Room 3, Cellular Energetics

Puzzle: ATP Audit

Enter net ATP yields per glucose:

  • Glycolysis: 2 ATP (cytosol)
  • Pyruvate Oxidation + Krebs: 2 ATP (matrix)
  • Electron Transport + Chemiosmosis: ~28 ATP (inner mitochondrial membrane). Accepts 26–34; textbooks vary depending on NADH shuttle efficiency.
  • Total: ~32 ATP

Final Question

The primary electron carrier that delivers high-energy electrons to the ETC is…

Answer: NADH → letter N

Alignment: AP Bio LO ENE-1.I / ENE-1.J ? ATP synthesis via chemiosmosis. NADH donates electrons to Complex I; FADH₂ to Complex II (~3 vs ~2 ATP per molecule).

Room 4, Cell Communication & Cell Cycle

Puzzle: Signal Cascade

Drag the 5 steps into the correct order for a G-protein-coupled receptor (GPCR) cascade, using epinephrine → glycogen breakdown:

  1. Epinephrine (ligand) binds to GPCR on cell surface
  2. Receptor changes shape and activates a G-protein inside the cell
  3. Activated G-protein activates adenylyl cyclase, producing cAMP (second messenger)
  4. cAMP activates Protein Kinase A, which phosphorylates enzymes
  5. Glycogen phosphorylase breaks down glycogen → glucose released

Final Question

General name for a biological catalyst that lowers activation energy?

Answer: enzyme → letter E

Alignment with AP Bio LO IST-3.A, Signal transduction pathways. The cascade illustrates signal amplification, where one hormone molecule can activate thousands of PKA molecules.

Room 5, Heredity

Puzzle: Punnett Safe

Complete a Punnett square for Pp × pp:

  • Offspring genotypes: Pp, Pp, pp, pp
  • Phenotype ratio: 1 purple : 1 white (1:1)

Final Question

A cross between an organism with a dominant phenotype (unknown genotype) and a homozygous recessive individual is called a…

Answer: testcross → letter T

Alignment with AP Bio LO IST-1.I, Mendelian inheritance. A 1:1 ratio in the offspring reveals the unknown parent was heterozygous; a 1:0 ratio (all dominant) would indicate homozygous dominant.

Room 6, Gene Expression & Regulation

Puzzle: Splice & Decode

The pre-mRNA contains 5 exons and 3 introns (introns start with GU, ending with GU/GC per the classic GT-AG rule). Students click each intron to remove it.

  • Exons to keep: AUG, GAU, AAU, GCA, UAA
  • Introns to remove: GUAAAG, GUACAG, GUAUAG (each begins with 5'-GU and ends with AG-3' per the canonical GT-AG splice rule)
  • Mature mRNA: AUG-GAU-AAU-GCA-UAA
  • Amino acids: Met – Asp (D) – Asn (N) – Ala (A) – STOP → spells "D-N-A" (fun Easter egg)

Final Question

The non-coding sequences removed from pre-mRNA are called…

Answer: introns → letter I

Alignment with AP Bio LO IST-1.K / IST-1.L, RNA processing. The spliceosome, a ribonucleoprotein complex, removes introns and ligates exons to form the mature mRNA before export from the nucleus.

Room 7, Natural Selection

Puzzle: Evolutionary Tree

Given a trait matrix with 5 species, pick the correct tree. Traits are cumulative: Lancelet (outgroup, no traits) → Lamprey (+vertebrae) → Salamander (+four limbs) → Lizard (+amniotic egg) → Mouse (+hair). The correct tree nests each new clade inside the previous:

Correct tree: Tree A (nested clades matching synapomorphies)

Final Question

A branching diagram showing evolutionary relationships based on shared derived traits is a…

Answer: cladogram → letter C

Alignment with AP Bio LO EVO-3.A, Constructing phylogenetic trees & cladograms. Parsimony principle: the simplest tree (fewest evolutionary changes) is preferred when multiple are consistent with the data.

Room 8, Ecology

Puzzle: Ecosystem Reset

Order stages of primary succession (post-volcanic eruption):

  1. 🪨 Bare rock exposed by eruption
  2. 🟢 Lichens and mosses colonize and soil begins forming (pioneer species)
  3. 🌱 Grasses and shrubs establish in soil
  4. 🌳 Mature forest community (climax)

Final Question

The gradual process of community change leading toward a stable climax community is ecological…

Answer: succession → letter S

Alignment with AP Bio LO SYI-3.F, Ecological community dynamics. Primary succession begins without soil (rock, glaciated surfaces); secondary succession begins after disturbance to existing soil (fire, flood, logging).
